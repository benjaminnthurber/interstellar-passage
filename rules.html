Rules of Passage

Read them once. Remember them forever.

  1. Payment Secures the Seat, Not the Outcome. Passage guarantees transport, not comfort, timing, or docking proximity. If you arrive close enough to walk away, the job is considered complete.
  2. Ask No Questions You Don’t Want Answers To. Routes, cargo histories, and crew backgrounds are not discussed. Curiosity has a higher fare than credits.
  3. Departure Times Are Suggestions. Ships leave when lanes clear, scans pass, and captains decide it’s time. Miss it, and the stars won’t wait.
  4. What Happens in Transit Stays in Transit. No logs. No records. No official acknowledgment that you were ever aboard.
  5. Carry Only What You Can Explain Quickly. If security asks, you answer. If they don’t ask, you still might want the story ready.
  6. Names Are Optional. The name you give is the name we use—for the duration of the trip only.
  7. No Refunds, No Retries. Once engines spool, the fare is spent. Turning back costs more than most people expect.
  8. If the Destination Changes, So Be It. Lanes shift. Beacons fail. Sometimes the safest port isn’t the one you paid for.

Rules subject to change without notice or explanation.
